Binders Boutique

Try it out

CHALLENGE

Design a microsite for a Ponce City Market retailer of your choice. This microsite will focus on selling a single product category consisting of 75-100 products. This microsite will target one of three specific personas

RESTRICTIONS

  • DO NOT offer any search functionality
  • DO NOT offer wizard-like “product finder” tools that bypass navigation
  • DO NOT offer online activities unrelated to purchasing products

PROCESS

My first step was to do competitive analysis of all the best pens websites out there. A lot of them had features that were unique like comprehensive filters — right down to if the ink bleeds, or if the ergonomics were for a left/right handed person.

With this project, it was the first time I was introduced to card-sorting, of which its value really depends on gathering the right users to test with. I was really lucky to get a mixed bunch of non-pen experts to professional illustrators who made really good suggestions on how to filter pens (people would actually search for ink properties like fast-drying or water-resistance).

From here, I dove into making my first mistake: emulating features as a way to solve problems rather than focusing on flow of navigation first. This reflects in the sketches and wireframes where I was so obsessed with coming up with the "cool factor" and tried to make tools like a interactive line-weight pen tester.

Having some developer skills, I modeled my website after a bootstrap theme while introducing a cool carousel product gallery. Check it out here. I realized with this version that it was comparable with a full-fledged website. As cool as the carousel was, I had feedback on how it was a little too "gimicky" it was and it didn't really emphasis that this was a lesser microsite to supplement the parent website.

RESULTS

After doing some user testing with people who were more familiar with microsites, I modified my iteration to focus on simplicity instead. This was also a point where I kept my persona in mind and concentrated on:

  • Making sure products were easy to find
  • Clean, intuitive site navigation
  • Empower users with knowledge about pens

WHAT I LEARNED

I had a bad case of "featuritis" with this project and learned how valuable personas are to keep me on track. I made the beginner mistake of designing for myself, but had to beat the class chants of "we are NOT the user!" into my head to focus my iterations.

TOOLS
Google Forms, HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, Javascript, Keynote, Quicktime Screen Recording
