2017 Marta Hackathon

Try it out

CHALLENGE

  • To increase ridership on MARTA
  • To improve the rider experience on MARTA

MY ROLE

This being my fourth hackathon, I took it on myself to guide others and plan out a strategy. I participated as a UX designer this time around instead of as a developer. I worked on heuristics and competitive analyses and created a survey to reaffirm our hypothesis regarding the impact rewards integrated into the MARTA app

APPROACH

Having finished my capstone project witht the "Sprint" methodology by Google Ventures, I wanted to try a compressed adaptation of that for this 24-hour hackathon. After forming groups, we did a competitive, comparative, and heuristics analyses to identify pain points. We wanted to reaffirm our hypothesis that a rewards-integration to the existing MARTA app is something people wanted — so we sent out a survey and called it a night.

PROCESS

Out of the nearly 200+ people, we got about 21 results back from our survey. After reading the results, I coordinated how our hours are to be spent the rest of the day. It was something like:

  • 9-10: Sketch ideas
  • 11-12: Combine ideas, start designin in SketchApp
  • 12-1: Eat lunch, network, talk to people for ideas
  • 1-2: Polish up designs, discuss information architecture if it makes sense. Other people on the team would start integrating our survey results into a Keynote presentation
  • 3-4: Export to InVision for prototyping while finishing up presentation
  • 4-5: Present to 3 different judges

RESULTS

We were able to accomplish our goals in a timely manner: have a clickable prototype, presentation materials, a team member to present the idea, all while maintaining our sanity and having fun. This hackathon was a great test to reaffirm the lessons I learned at General Assembly and reflect how well I can work with others – especially people I just met.
